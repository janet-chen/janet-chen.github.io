Hi, I’m a 1st year PhD student in Information Science at Cornell. I earned my BA in computer science and economics at the University of British Columbia. My BA had a co-op program, which means I spent about 12 months at startups as a software engineering intern.

I’m advised by Dr. Qian Yang. I’m interested in designing data-driven technologies using intimate data and mediating intimacy. Some of my past work touches on preparing digital data in preparation of death, designing for possible data management futures, and creating AR/VR applications.

In my spare time, I enjoy reading self-help books (got any recommendations? ), climbing, and being outside. I’m happy to answer questions or point to recommendations about grad school, admissions, and Cornell for prospective students. In particular, I welcome questions from underrepresented minorities.
Interests: Human-computer interaction, interaction design, designing with AI

Projects

  • Trauma-informed Design [Jan 2021 - Present] Advised by Nicola Dell and Thomas Ristenpart. In collaboration with NortonLifeLock and UMichigan. Supervised Spring'21 research for masters students Rui Maki and Jeremy Hazan.
  • Understanding intimate data's potential as a design material [Oct 2020 - Present] Advised by Qian Yang. Supervised Spring'21 research term for Cornell undergrads and MPS students: Emily Chan, Connie Liu, Meryl Ye, and Emma Wang.
  • Designing for Death and Data [Jan - Aug 2020] Advised by Francesco Vitale and Joanna McGrenere. We all have copious amounts of data that won’t be easy to deal with when we’re dying (or dead). How can novel prototypes support preparing data for death? We created 12 design concepts to explore promising approaches, and what to avoid.
  • Personalized Data Management [Sep - Dec 2019] Assisting Francesco Vitale. Advised by Joanna McGrenere. Thinking about how centralization and customization can help people manage their overwhelming amount of data.
  • Developing Design Guidelines for AR-based Assembly Instructions [Jan - Aug 2019] Advised by Dongwook Yoon. We worked on developing robust and useful design guidelines for AR-based assembly instructions. Think IKEA instructions but through a HoloLens. For this project, I interviewed AR experts, used Unity to code AR protoypes, created an interactive website, and got my first taste of research.
